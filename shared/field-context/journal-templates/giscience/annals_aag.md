# Annals of the American Association of Geographers
## Paper Template & Formatting Guide

**Publisher**: Taylor & Francis / AAG
**Impact Factor**: ~4
**Scope**: Human geography, physical geography, GIScience, geographic theory

---

## Submission Requirements

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|--------------|
| Word limit | 9,000 words (including references) |
| Abstract | 150 words |
| Keywords | 5–7 |
| References | APA 7th edition |
| Format | Double-spaced, 12pt Times New Roman |
| Figures | ≤8 figures; 300 DPI |

---

## Paper Structure

### Abstract (≤150 words)
Include: context, research question, methods, key findings, geographic/theoretical contribution.

### Introduction
- Geographic/theoretical framing (broader than methods-only journals)
- Research question embedded in theoretical context
- Contributions to geographic thought, not just methods

### Conceptual/Theoretical Framework
Annals values theoretical grounding. This section situates your work within geographic theory.

### Study Area
- Geographic context, history, why geographically significant
- Political/administrative boundaries relevant to the study
- Photographs or historical context if appropriate

### Data and Methods
- Data sources with access information
- Methods described for geographically diverse readership (not only specialists)
- Positionality statement if qualitative/mixed methods

### Results
- Geographic interpretation emphasized
- Maps should show patterns at relevant scales
- Statistical results contextualized geographically

### Discussion
- Engage with geographic theory
- Broader implications for geographic understanding
- Policy relevance

### Conclusions
- Theoretical contribution
- Methodological contribution
- Practical/policy contribution
- Future research agenda

---

## Annals-Specific Notes

- Geography journals value **place** and **context** — always situate findings geographically
- Theoretical engagement is expected, not optional
- Mixed-methods approaches welcomed
- Human geography context should be acknowledged even in technical papers

## Checklist

- [ ] Abstract ≤150 words
- [ ] Theoretical framework section included
- [ ] Positionality statement (if applicable)
- [ ] Geographic interpretation emphasized throughout
