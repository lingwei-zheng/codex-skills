# Human Final Pass Calibration

## Two independent dimensions

Naturalness and grammatical correctness are separate controls.

- `Formal + grammatical`: standard academic prose.
- `Natural + grammatical`: more author-like but still correct.
- `Formal + imperfect`: only possible when grammar-error mode is explicitly enabled.
- `Natural + imperfect`: also only possible when grammar-error mode is explicitly enabled.

## Naturalness target

Aim for roughly 70% formal academic expression and 30% restrained natural expression within editable prose in eligible sections.

This is a style guide, not a quota. Estimate at the sentence or clause level after editing.

If the paper would lose rigor at that density, reduce the naturalness share and note that decision in `Revision notes:`.

## Calibration procedure

1. Count only editable prose in eligible sections.
2. Mark sentences or clauses as `formal` or `natural`.
3. Keep `natural` near 30% of the editable material.
4. Preserve already-human passages as part of the natural share without forcing extra changes.

## Suggested distribution across sections

- Introduction: 35-45% of human-style edits
- Related Work or equivalent: 20-30%
- Results: 30-40%

Use this distribution flexibly when one section is short or absent.

## Grammar-error overlay

When grammar-error mode is explicitly enabled:

- target about 1 imperfection per 250-400 editable words;
- cap at 6 imperfections per paper unless the user asks for a different rate;
- imperfections may sit on either a formal or natural sentence;
- grammar imperfections are not the main humanization mechanism.
