# Ratio Calibration

## Two Independent Dimensions

Naturalness and grammatical correctness are orthogonal. A sentence can be:

- Formal + Grammatical: standard academic prose.
- Natural + Grammatical: conversational academic style, grammatically correct.
- Formal + Imperfect: academic style with an intentional minor grammar flaw.
- Natural + Imperfect: conversational style with an intentional minor grammar flaw.

Calibrate these two dimensions independently:

| Dimension | Target |
|:---|:---|
| Naturalness (Formal → Natural) | ~30% of editable sentences in eligible sections should be Natural |
| Grammar (Correct → Imperfect) | Only when grammar-error mode is enabled; 1 per 250-400 words, max 6 per paper |

## Target Ratio (Naturalness)

Aim for approximately 70% formal academic expression and 30% restrained natural or conversational expression within editable prose in the eligible sections.

This ratio is a style guide, not a word-count guarantee. Estimate it at the sentence or clause level after editing.

When grammar-error mode is enabled, grammar imperfections are layered independently on top of naturalness. The 70/30 ratio governs the Formal/Natural split only. Imperfections are counted and capped separately (see Grammar-Imperfection Ratios below).

## Calibration Procedure

1. Count only editable prose in eligible sections.
2. Mark sentences as:
   - Formal: standard academic phrasing with no noticeable conversational tone.
   - Natural: still academic, but more direct, varied, or lightly conversational.
3. Keep natural sentences near 30% of editable sentences.
4. If grammar-error mode is enabled, mark natural and formal sentences that carry an imperfection separately. An imperfection does not change a Natural sentence to Formal or vice versa; it is an independent overlay.

### Grammar-Imperfection Ratios (Independent Overlay)

When grammar-error mode is enabled:
- Target density: 1 imperfection per 250-400 editable words.
- Cap: 6 imperfections per full paper unless the user requests another rate.
- An imperfection may land on either a Formal or a Natural sentence. Either is valid.
- Grammar imperfections are not the main mechanism for humanization; natural phrasing density drives the human feel.

## Suggested Distribution

- Introduction: 35-45% of human-style edits.
- Related Work or Related Works: 20-30% of human-style edits.
- Results: 30-40% of human-style edits.

Use the distribution flexibly when one target section is short or absent.

## Quality Floor

If the 3:7 ratio would make the paper less rigorous, reduce the natural phrasing density and report that choice. Academic credibility has priority over exact ratio matching.
