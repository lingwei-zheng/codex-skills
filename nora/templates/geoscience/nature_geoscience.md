# Nature Geoscience
## Paper Template & Formatting Guide

**Publisher**: Nature Portfolio / Springer Nature
**Impact Factor**: ~19
**Scope**: Earth sciences — broad significance, landmark findings

---

## Submission Requirements

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|--------------|
| Article types | Article (3,000 words), Letter (deprecated), Brief Communication |
| Word limit | 3,000 words (main text) |
| Abstract | 200 words, no citations, no structured headings |
| Figures | ≤8 for articles |
| References | ≤50 for articles |
| Methods | Separate "Methods" section (not counted in word limit) |
| Extended Data | Up to 10 figures/tables (not in word limit) |

---

## Nature Geoscience Article Structure

### Abstract (≤200 words)
No headings. No citations. Lead with the scientific problem, then findings, then implications.
The abstract must be accessible to all scientists, not just geoscientists.

### Main Text (~3,000 words)
Nature does NOT use numbered sections. Instead, use flowing paragraphs with natural transitions.

Typical flow:
1. Opening paragraph: importance and context
2. Summary of results (key finding stated upfront)
3. Methods overview (brief — details in Methods section)
4. Detailed results discussion
5. Broader implications

### Methods (separate, uncounted)
Full technical details, datasets, model parameters, statistical tests.
Must be fully reproducible.

### Extended Data (up to 10 items)
Additional figures, tables, and data not in main text.

### Supplementary Information
Code, raw data, lengthy derivations.

---

## Nature-Specific Writing Style

- **Lead with the finding**: Never bury the result in paragraph 4
- **Accessible language**: Broader audience than specialist journals
- **Significance statement**: Must communicate why this matters beyond the subfield
- **No jargon without definition**
- **Active voice preferred**

## Pre-submission Enquiry

Nature journals strongly recommend a pre-submission enquiry (editor check) before full submission.
Include: 1-paragraph summary, key finding, why Nature Geoscience specifically.

## Checklist

- [ ] Abstract ≤200 words, no citations
- [ ] Main text ≤3,000 words
- [ ] Methods section complete and reproducible
- [ ] Extended Data ≤10 items
- [ ] Data available statement included
- [ ] Code deposited (GitHub + Zenodo DOI)
- [ ] Author contributions (CRediT)
- [ ] Competing interests statement
