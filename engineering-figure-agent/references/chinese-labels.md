# Chinese Labels

Use this reference when the requested figure contains Chinese text.

## Rules

- Chinese labels are allowed directly in the image when the user wants them.
- Keep Chinese labels concise, clear, and readable.
- Avoid paragraph-length Chinese text inside figures unless explicitly requested.
- Use larger label regions, stronger spacing, and higher contrast when there are many Chinese labels.
- Preserve standard English symbols, formula variables, protocol names, model names, and abbreviations when they improve technical clarity.
- Do not force awkward full-Chinese replacements for notation such as FFT, CNN, pH, loss, IoU, x, y, t, or sigma.
- For exact charts, prefer local plotting so Chinese labels and geometry remain controllable.

## Prompt Phrases

- `use concise, clear Chinese labels`
- `avoid dense Chinese paragraph text`
- `preserve standard English symbols and formula variables where appropriate`
- `keep labels horizontally aligned and centered within boxes`
- `white background, academic vector-like style, readable Chinese labels`
- `large clean text regions, centered Chinese labels, balanced spacing`

## Safer Patterns

- `Generate the full academic diagram structure with readable Chinese labels, balanced spacing, and clean alignment.`
- `Use Chinese for descriptive labels, but preserve standard English symbols, abbreviations, and formula variables where technically appropriate.`
- `For chart-like figures, emphasize accurate geometry and keep Chinese labels readable and uncluttered.`
